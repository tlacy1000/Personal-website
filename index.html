You're probably wondering how I got from there to here...

When I was 18 years old, I had a strong interest in science. There were so many things I was interested in- biology, psychology, physiology, chemistry. I had no idea what exactly I wanted to do, until I found the perfect solution- something that incorporated all of these! The wonderful, multidisciplinary world of: Neuroscience! I did my undergrad in behavioral neuroscience- a specialty of neuro that focuses on the application of the principles of biology to the study of physiological, genetic, and developmental mechanisms of behavior in humans and other animals. Throughout the years I worked in home health nursing, long term care facilities, hospitals, and various research labs. I had the time of my life learning all about the brain and what makes it tick- pretty cool to use an organ to study itself! After graduation, I sought to refine my interests. I knew I had a strong interest in the applications of learning, data science, and software development. When I learned about UT's Austin software engineering and web development program, I knew I'd found my calling. These days, I've completed the program but am continually refining my skills so that I can be an ever improving software developer. I'm most interested in combining my skills as a scientist with my newfound passion for being a developer and analyst. I was previously working as a database analyst for an environmental conservation company, but am currently a surgical neurophysiologist.
